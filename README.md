My research interests center on robotics, with an emphasis on motion and task planning in complex, open environments.  
Through my research experiences, I have come to believe that meaningful robot autonomy requires bridging classical planning and optimization methods with learning-based approaches, grounded in physical interaction with the real world.  
I am particularly interested in developing hybrid planning frameworks that combine the strong feasibility, safety, and efficiency guarantees of classical methods with the adaptability of learning-based models under sensing noise, model mismatch, and environmental uncertainty, with the goal of improving the robustness, reliability, and interpretability of robotic decision-making systems for safer and more practical real-world deployment.

In the long term, I aim to contribute to embodied intelligence by developing robots that can generalize from limited supervision and adapt to novel tasks and environments.  
I am always open to research collaborations and research-oriented opportunities in robotics and artificial intelligence.
